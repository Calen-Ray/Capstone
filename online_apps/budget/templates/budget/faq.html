The frequently asked questions FAQ section


  • "Are there any promotional material in this website?"

    No, there are no collaborations, sponsored links, or non federally accumulated base-data. Budget bird is designed to provide a budget template and resources for those who desire.

  • "Where do you get data on national average costs based on income level?"

    So glad you asked. The data was semi-painstakingly aquired from the US bureau of labor statistics, they have a wonderful section of national averages on expendature you can sort by income earned pre tax.

  • "I dont agree with some of the material posted."

    Entirely understandable, personal finance is in fact... personal. If you have legitimate concerns of data or documents from the library please reachout to me on the contact page.

  • "How do you calculate the local cost of living pricing?"

    This is extrapolated from the national average data multiplied by whats called the 'cost of living index' in each city. For the nerdy out there it looks something like this:
    {'name': 'Portland', 'index': 134.7, 'grocery': 113.1, 'housing': 184.1, 'utilities': 88.5, 'transportation': 128.4, 'healthcare': 117.1, 'misc': 121.0}
    Where you take the national average as 100 points, so in Portland, its an average of 34.7% higher general cost of living, or 84.1% higher in cost of housing, 13.1% higher in groceries. etc

  • "Will the data continue to be updated?"

    Unless this tool becomes popular, I will likely leave the data as-is. 2019-2021 compiled.

  • "Can I share my budget code?"

    Yes, feel free to share your budget code, but keep the pin secret to allow you to be the only one who can edit.
